About

Welcome to our brain stroke predictor!

We all have been affected by someone we know who has had a stroke, and we wanted to determine if we could understand what varying factors play a role in whether an individual is more or less likely to be at risk for having a stroke.

🧠 Strokes are the 2nd leading cause of death globally, responsible for approximately 11% of total deaths.

🧠When deprived of blood and the oxygen it carries, or when bleeding saturates the surrounding tissue causing the brain to swell, the brain’s operation becomes compromised.

🧠Can cause long-term vision problems, seizures, fatigue, loss of speech, memory loss, and paralysis among other adverse effects. They can also cause death.

Based on the following factors: Gender, Age, Hypertension, Heart Disease Status, Marital Status, Work Type, Residence, Avg Glucose Level, BMI, and Smoking Status can we predict whether or not an individual is more at risk for having a stroke?

Machine Learning

To determine which machine learning model to use we tested out three different supervised machine on our dataset and observed how well each machine performed. The three machines we tested were a logistic regression model, a random forest model and a support vector machine. Of the three models tested the random forest performed the best. From the picture below we can see that the random forest performed with 99% accuracy.



The data was resampled using an oversampling so that the number of stroke occurrences were the same as the number of non-stroke occurrences. X and Y were then split into training and testing sets. The x variable used the following columns for gender, age, hypertension, heart disease, marital status, work type, residence type, average glucose level, bmi, and smoking status. The y variable used the stroke column to determine whether or not a person was more at risk for having a stroke.

Conclusion

Our data showed that people who have heart disease or hypertension are more likely to have a stroke. Woman and men had a similar percentage of those who suffered a stroke. A higher percentage of those who are self-employed had a stroke, which could indicate that not having a fixed salary can suffer from increased risk of a stroke and people who smoke and formerly smoked are those who had a higher percentage of having a stroke because smoking is a cause of short and long term diseases.

Our data set didn’t include other potential factors of stroke like drug use, alcohol consumption, and family history of strokes. Collecting and analyzing additional factors would help our machine learning model in determining if other individuals are at potential risk of stroke. Also, our data set didn’t have a high number of those who have had a stroke, causing us to use oversampling creating artificial data for the machine learning model.
